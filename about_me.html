About Me

I also write on Medium about small projects, in case you're interested.

Since I was a child, technology has been a big passion, from the toys I preffered to the TV shows I watched and games I played. A few years passed and now I'm a Computer Engineering undergrad student at UFSCar. After a personal project with an Arduino, I realized that coding was all that I've been looking for all my life. Ever since, I've dedicated myself to learn coding as much as possible.

After learning a bit of python, I completelly fell in love with the language, taking my time to understand most of it's ins and outs. As of today I use the web-framework 'Django' for most of my projects, wich ended up teaching me about SQL, Docker, and much more. By deploying some of the personal projects at my home server, I've even dug a bit into some DevOps. The fact that all of this knolage is just 'up for grabs' in the internet just facinates me, making it simpler than ever to learn.

I've found myself seeing beauty in the 'Art of Code', realizing that a simple, but effective solution is often much better and more appropriate than a complex one with unneeded functionallity. In other words, I like to keep it simple.

Even recently, I've dug a bit into the world of DevOps, while managing a Raspberry Pi, or my little 'Docker Pi' as I like to call it. She serves my personal projects in a much better way than expected. I write about small project with my home-server in Medium.
